Bread Buns

Bread buns, a staple in bakeries and kitchens worldwide, are beloved for their soft texture, versatility, and comforting aroma. Originating from Europe, these delightful pastries have evolved over centuries, with each culture adding its unique twist.

Before diving into the delightful world of bread bun creation, it's essential to note potential allergens like wheat, gluten, and yeast. For those with sensitivities, alternative flours and yeast-free recipes can be explored. Additionally, ensuring precise measurements and kneading techniques will guarantee fluffy, perfectly risen buns.

Prep time

35 min.

Cook time

15 min.

Servings

12 bread buns

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dough: In a large mixing bowl, combine flour, yeast, sugar, and salt. Gradually add warm water and knead until a smooth dough forms.
  2. Let the Dough Rise: Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel and let the dough rise in a warm place until it doubles in size, usually about 1 hour.
  3. Shape the Buns: Punch down the risen dough and divide it into equal-sized portions. Shape each portion into a round bun and place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  4. Second Rise: Cover the buns with a kitchen towel and let them rise for another 30 minutes to an hour until they double in size again.
  5. Bake:Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Brush the buns with egg wash (optional) and bake for 15-20 minutes or until they turn golden brown.
  6. Cool and Enjoy: Allow the buns to cool on a wire rack before serving. Enjoy them warm or store them in an airtight container for later.

Ingredients:

  1. 3 cups all-purpose flour
  2. 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast
  3. 2 tablespoons sugar
  4. 1 teaspoon salt
  5. 1 cup warm water
  6. Egg wash (optional)
