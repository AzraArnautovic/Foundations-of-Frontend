Vegan Buddha Bowl

A Vegan Buddha Bowl is a nourishing and satisfying meal that typically consists of a variety of plant-based ingredients, such as grains, vegetables, legumes, and healthy fats, all served together in a bowl. It's called a Buddha Bowl because of its rounded shape, which resembles the belly of a Buddha statue.

These bowls are not only visually appealing but also packed with nutrients and flavor. They offer endless possibilities for creativity and customization, making them a favorite among vegans and health-conscious eaters.

Prep time

20 min.

Cook time

15 min.

Servings

4 servings

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Ingredients: Gather your choice of grains (quinoa, rice, or couscous), a variety of vegetables (such as broccoli, carrots, bell peppers, and spinach), chickpeas or tofu for protein, and your favorite sauce or dressing.
  2. Cook the Grains: Cook the grains according to package instructions. You can use a rice cooker or stovetop method.
  3. Prepare the Vegetables: Wash and chop the vegetables into bite-sized pieces. You can steam, roast, or saut√© them depending on your preference.
  4. Cook the Protein: If using chickpeas, drain and rinse them. If using tofu, press it to remove excess water, then dice it into cubes. Cook the protein in a skillet with a little oil until lightly browned and heated through.
  5. Assemble the Bowls: Divide the cooked grains, vegetables, and protein among serving bowls. Arrange them in sections for visual appeal.
  6. Drizzle with Sauce: Drizzle your favorite sauce or dressing over the bowls. You can use tahini, soy sauce, peanut sauce, or any other dressing you prefer.
  7. Enjoy: Serve your vegan Buddha bowls immediately and enjoy a nutritious and satisfying meal!

Ingredients:

  1. 2 cups cooked grains (quinoa, rice, or couscous)
  2. Assorted vegetables (broccoli, carrots, bell peppers, spinach, etc.)
  3. 1 can chickpeas or 1 block tofu
  4. Your choice of sauce or dressing
