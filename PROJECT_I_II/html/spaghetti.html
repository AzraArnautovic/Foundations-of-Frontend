Spaghetti Bolognese

Spaghetti Bolognese is a classic Italian pasta dish loved by many for its rich, meaty sauce and comforting flavors. Originating from the Bologna region of Italy, this dish has become a staple in households worldwide.

With a few simple ingredients and a bit of time, you can create a delicious and satisfying meal that the whole family will enjoy.

Prep time

10 min.

Cook time

1 hr

Servings

4 servings

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti and cook according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. Prepare the Sauce: In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onions, carrots, and celery, and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.
  3. Brown the Meat: Add ground beef to the skillet and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks. Drain excess fat if needed.
  4. Simmer: Stir in tomato paste, crushed tomatoes, beef broth, Worcestershire sauce, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat and let it simmer for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and flavors meld together.
  5. Serve: Serve the Bolognese sauce over cooked spaghetti, garnish with grated Parmesan cheese and fresh basil if desired. Enjoy!

Ingredients:

  1. 8 oz spaghetti
  2. 1 lb ground beef
  3. 1 onion, finely chopped
  4. 1 carrot, finely chopped
  5. 1 celery stalk, finely chopped
  6. 2 cloves garlic, minced
  7. 2 tablespoons olive oil
  8. 2 tablespoons olive oil
  9. 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  10. 1 (14 oz) can crushed tomatoes
  11. 1 cup beef broth
  12. 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  13. 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  14. 1/2 teaspoon salt, or to taste
  15. 1/4 teaspoon black pepper, or to taste
